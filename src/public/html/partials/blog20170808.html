Philadelphia's Emissions Targets

Mayor Jim Kenney has set emissions targets for Philadelphia that mimic the United States' Paris agreement, which is 26-28% by 2025. Philadelphia also has a long term commitment of 80% by 2050. I'd like to explore these targets a bit, both in their adequacy and the difficulty Philadelphia might have in reaching them.

In the last post I argued that the US Paris commitments were not consistent with the headline goal of the Paris agreement. This is without even considering the equity argument; that is, those of us with large footprints, using up more of the budget, ought to reduce our emissions the fastest. It is far easier for us to give up a flight per year, or to switch our diets from beef to chicken, than it is for the global poor to eliminate their already tiny amounts of carbon dioxide.
